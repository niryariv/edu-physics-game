🔵 Newton's First Law ‚Äî Inertia

"If something is NOT moving, it will stay still. If something IS moving, it will keep moving at the same speed in the same direction ‚Äî FOREVER ‚Äî unless something pushes or pulls it. That resistance to change is called INERTIA! üèí"

Think of it this way: objects are LAZY! They don't want to start moving, and once they are moving, they don't want to stop. Inertia is just a fancy word for that laziness.

  • üöó Seatbelts: When a car brakes hard, your body keeps flying forward because of inertia. The seatbelt grabs you and applies the stopping force.
  • üèí Hockey puck on ice: Ice is very slippery (low friction), so there's almost nothing to slow the puck ‚Äî it keeps sliding and sliding!
  • üöÄ Spaceship in space: There's no air in space, so nothing slows a moving spaceship. It glides on FOREVER until a force acts on it.

🟠 Newton's Second Law ‚Äî F = ma

"Push something HARDER and it speeds up FASTER. Push something HEAVIER and it speeds up SLOWER. This is the formula: Force = Mass × Acceleration (or: a = F ÷ m) üöÄ"

Imagine pushing a shopping trolley. An empty trolley is easy to push fast. A trolley full of heavy shopping needs a much bigger push to get moving. That's this law in action! The same force gives a lighter object MORE acceleration.

  • ‚öΩ Same kick, different balls: Kick a light football and a heavy bowling ball with the same force. The football flies far; the bowling ball barely moves.
  • üèéÔ∏è Race cars have huge engines: Racing cars need enormous force to accelerate their mass from 0 to 200 km/h in just a few seconds.
  • üõí Shopping trolley: An empty trolley is easy to push fast. Fill it with shopping and the same push gives much less acceleration.

🟣 Newton's Third Law ‚Äî Action & Reaction

"Every push has an equal push back! If you push something, it pushes YOU back with the exact same strength ‚Äî but in the OPPOSITE direction! üöÄ"

Try pushing against a wall. You push the wall forward; the wall pushes YOU backward. You don't fall through the wall because the forces are equal and opposite. This is why rockets work ‚Äî they push gas backwards, and the gas pushes the rocket forwards!

  • üöÄ Rocket engines: Hot gas blasts downward (action). The gas pushes the rocket upward (reaction). Equal force, opposite direction!
  • üéà Balloon: Let go of an inflated balloon and air shoots out one end (action) ‚Äî the balloon flies the other way (reaction)!
  • üèä Swimming: You push water backward with your hands (action). The water pushes you forward (reaction).
  • üö£ Rowing a boat: You push the water back with the oar (action). The water pushes the boat forward (reaction).

‚ö° Conservation of Energy

Energy cannot be created or destroyed ‚Äî only transformed!

Energy is like a currency that never disappears ‚Äî it only gets exchanged for a different type! ‚ö°

Potential energy is energy that's stored and waiting ‚Äî like a ball at the top of a hill, a stretched rubber band, or a battery. Kinetic energy is energy that's moving ‚Äî like a rolling ball, a flying plane, or a running cheetah.

When the ball rolls down the hill, every bit of potential energy turns into kinetic energy. At the very bottom, it's ALL kinetic. At the very top, it's ALL potential. In between, it's a mix ‚Äî but the TOTAL always stays the same. That's conservation of energy!

üåç Gravity & Projectile Motion

Every thrown object follows a perfect parabola!

Gravity is a force that pulls every object toward Earth's centre. It gives everything an acceleration of 9.8 m/s¬≤ ‚Äî that means every second you fall, you're going 9.8 metres per second faster! üåç

When you throw a ball, TWO things happen at once: it moves sideways because you threw it, AND it moves downward because gravity is always pulling. The combination of these two movements creates the curved path called a parabola ‚Äî that's every throw, kick, or shot you've ever seen!

The angle you throw at matters a lot. Throw too flat and it hits the ground before going far. Throw too steep and it goes mostly up. The sweet spot is 45¬∞ ‚Äî perfectly balanced between height and distance.
