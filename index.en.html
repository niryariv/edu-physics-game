🔵 Newton's First Law — Inertia

"If something is NOT moving, it will stay still. If something IS moving, it will keep moving at the same speed in the same direction — FOREVER — unless something pushes or pulls it. That resistance to change is called INERTIA! 🏈"

Think of it this way: objects are LAZY! They don't want to start moving, and once they are moving, they don't want to stop. Inertia is just a fancy word for that laziness.

  • 🚗 Seatbelts: When a car brakes hard, your body keeps flying forward because of inertia.
  • 🏈 Hockey puck on ice: Ice is very slippery (low friction), so the puck keeps sliding!
  • 🚀 Spaceship in space: There's no air in space, so nothing slows a moving spaceship. It glides on FOREVER.

🟠 Newton's Second Law — F = ma

"Push something HARDER and it speeds up FASTER. Push something HEAVIER and it speeds up SLOWER. Formula: Force = Mass × Acceleration (or: a = F ÷ m) 🚀"

Imagine pushing a shopping trolley. An empty trolley is easy to push fast. A trolley full of heavy shopping needs a much bigger push to get moving. The same force gives a lighter object MORE acceleration.

  • ⚽ Same kick, different balls: Kick a light football and a heavy bowling ball with the same force. The football flies far; the bowling ball barely moves.
  • 🏎️ Race cars: Racing cars need enormous force to accelerate from 0 to 200 km/h in just a few seconds.
  • 🛒 Shopping trolley: An empty trolley is easy. Fill it with shopping and the same push gives much less acceleration.
📚 In Law 1, FRICTION was the force that slowed your puck — now in Law 2 you choose the force directly. F = ma shows exactly how forces change velocity!

🟣 Newton's Third Law — Action & Reaction

"Every push has an equal push back! If you push something, it pushes YOU back with the exact same strength — but in the OPPOSITE direction! 🚀"

Try pushing against a wall. You push the wall forward; the wall pushes YOU backward. This is why rockets work — they push gas backwards, and the gas pushes the rocket forwards!

  • 🚀 Rocket engines: Hot gas blasts downward (action). The gas pushes the rocket upward (reaction).
  • 🎈 Balloon: Air shoots out one end (action) — the balloon flies the other way (reaction)!
  • 🏈 Swimming: You push water backward (action). The water pushes you forward (reaction).

⚡ Conservation of Energy

Energy cannot be created or destroyed — only transformed!

Energy is like a currency that never disappears — it only gets exchanged for a different type!

Potential energy is stored energy — like a ball at the top of a hill. Kinetic energy is energy of movement. When the ball rolls down, every bit of PE turns into KE. The TOTAL always stays the same.

📚 In Law 3, the rocket burned fuel to gain speed. That speed IS kinetic energy (½mv²)!

🌍 Gravity & Projectile Motion

Every thrown object follows a perfect parabola!

Gravity pulls every object toward Earth with acceleration 9.8 m/s². When you throw a ball, it moves sideways from your throw AND downward from gravity — the combination creates a parabola.

The angle you throw at matters a lot. The sweet spot is 45° — perfectly balanced between height and distance.

📚 In Energy, stored height became launch speed. That same v₀ is your projectile's starting velocity!
